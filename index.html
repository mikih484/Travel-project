Why Bratislava?

Bratislava, "Little Big City"

Bratislava, the capital and largest city of Slovakia, is a charming, compact city located on the Danube River at the border with Austria and Hungary. Known for its pedestrian-only medieval Old Town, hilltop castle, and vibrant café culture, it features a unique mix of gothic, baroque, and communist-era architecture.Often called "Little Big City," it offers a relaxed, walkable experience compared to neighboring capitals. The city hosts various events, including the Bratislava Spring Music Festival and Uprising Festival. A compact Danube-side gem ideal for a 1-2 day trip.

Bratislava is also rich in folklore, featuring legends about giant wizards (Klingsor) turning the castle into a table, a maiden falling for an enemy Turkish chief, and a massive snake drinking blood beneath the castle. Famous city legends include a love-struck Napoleonic soldier, the ghost of a tragic maiden at Devin Castle, and a knight whose song inspired a magical fountain.

Múza v šálke

Múza v šálke is café with a unique concept - at the two large tables that will certainly catch your attention, you can make your own pottery. Not only that but everything is served in unique handmade pieces of potery made by the owner. It is a place full of life where creativity, comunity and relax atmosphere connect.

What I like about it

It has a unique charm that sparks creatiivity with a rich coffee rosted localy in Slovakia. In summer, you can enjoy terrace with small tables or sitting by the large open windows. The comfortable retro furnishing inside just invites you to sit and relax.
